# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Malaysian aesthetic-treatment seekers — predominantly women 25–55 researching a specific concern (acne, pigmentation, ageing, contouring) or treatment (HIFU, Pico, CoolSculpting) before committing. They arrive **mobile-first**, often mid-consideration from Google or an AI answer engine, comparing a handful of clinics. Their context is cautious: this is a medical decision about their face and body, so trust, credentials, and clarity matter more than hype. The job to be done: *understand whether this treatment/clinic is right for me, at a branch near me, then talk to someone* — which means a WhatsApp message to book a free consultation (the only conversion; there is no online booking).

## Product Purpose

kaiteki.my is the marketing and discovery surface for Kaiteki Skin Aesthetic Clinic — a 9-branch MOH-licensed Malaysian chain (KL, Selangor, Johor, Sabah). It exists to (1) win organic and AI-answer visibility for treatments, concerns, and locations, (2) establish medical trust (E-E-A-T: named MMC-registered doctors, KKLIU-compliant claims), and (3) convert that trust into WhatsApp consultations. Success = more qualified WhatsApp leads, better rankings than competitors (benchmark: cliniccleo.com), and full compliance with Malaysian medical-advertising law. The design IS the product: a visitor's impression in the first fold decides whether Kaiteki reads as a premium, credible clinic or another templated aesthetic site.

## Brand Personality

**Warm · assured · unhurried.** A boutique-spa sense of care fused with genuine medical authority — the feeling of a considered consultation room, not a hard sell. Voice is calm and plain-spoken (a doctor explaining, not an ad shouting); warmth comes from the material palette and generous space, credibility from named doctors and visible registrations. Emotional goal: *reassured*. The visitor should feel looked-after and in safe hands before they ever tap WhatsApp.

## Anti-references

- **Salon / glam** (gold foil, script fonts, aspirational before/after energy) — reads cheap *and* is a MAB compliance risk (testimonials/before-after are prohibited).
- **Sterile hospital** (cold clinical-white, institutional, blue-grey) — throws away the warmth the brand palette gives.
- **Generic SaaS / startup** (gradient-purple, Inter, hero-metric template, feature-card grids) — the AI-slop look; invisible.
- **Cluttered competitor** (cliniccleo.com, Beverly Wilshire) — dense, multi-CTA, busy; the opposite of the calm we want.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust is the aesthetic.** Credentials, MMC numbers, KKLIU references and branch NAP are treated as designed objects (the "ledger"), not fine print. The site should *look* governed and verifiable — the moat competitors lack.
2. **One calm path to WhatsApp.** A single, unmissable conversion action per view; the warm neutrals exist precisely so the one saturated green CTA owns the eye. Never compete with the CTA.
3. **Warmth carries the luxury; restraint carries the credibility.** Space, material palette, and photography do the indulgent work; layout stays uncluttered and legible so it never tips into salon-glam or overwhelm.
4. **Evidence over aspiration.** With before/after and testimonials off-limits, persuasion comes from real clinics, real devices, named doctors, and factual depth — not results claims.
5. **The law wins.** Where design ambition and Malaysian medical-advertising rules (MAB/KKLIU, MMC, PDPA) conflict, compliance decides. Non-negotiable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA is the baseline gate (enforced via Pa11y/axe in CI). Warm-neutral surfaces mean contrast is a live risk: body text uses only the espresso/derived-brown inks (≥4.5:1); sand/mocha/porcelain are surface/large-accent/border colours only, never small text. Mobile-first and thumb-reachable (Malaysian traffic is overwhelmingly mobile). `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on every animation. Content is English-first with the architecture ready for Chinese (`/zh`) later; `lang="en-MY"`.
